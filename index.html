I still love
you. I am
just tired of

This is a quiet scrapbook for all the words that never made it where they were supposed to go. It is not anger. It is not performance. It is love, memory, exhaustion, and one stubborn hope that maybe silence does not have to be the ending.

Loose pages

Memory Fragments

Half-sentences, private notes, things left in margins because they were too true to say plainly.

I still remember how ordinary days felt softer when you were in them.

Some people do not become memories. They become weather.

You were never easy to forget because you were never small to begin with.

I stopped talking about it because repetition gets humiliating after a point.

There is a special kind of loneliness in carrying two people's silence by yourself.

I do not miss the past exactly. I miss the version of me that was not this tired.

Some absences are quiet in public and unbearably loud in private.

If love survived this long, then perhaps what faded was only my strength to keep pretending.

Marked personal

The Truth

Just the sentence that should have been said plainly. "Communication is key ? idk maybe : ("

I still love you. I still miss you. But I am tired of being the only one carrying this whole graveyard of memories, questions, and unfinished conversations.

This was never about loving you less. It was about realising that love, when carried alone for too long, starts becoming weight instead of warmth. I kept preserving what we were as though it were sacred, and maybe it was. But even sacred things can wear a person down. So here it is, as directly as it deserves to be said: if there is anything left in your heart at all, reach out to me. God dammit.

— the one still waiting, but no longer pretending it is easy
Final spread

Last Page

Not closure. A final invitation. Those are different things.

What remains

Your absence is no longer dramatic. It is quieter than that now. It lives in the corner of old evenings, in songs I should have stopped replaying, in the pause after laughter, and in all the little places where memory refuses to behave. I do not think the feeling left. I think it simply learned how to sit still.

What I am asking

If this meant anything to you, if some part of you still turns back toward what we were, then stop letting silence do all the talking. Reach out to me. God dammit. Not because the past can be repeated, but because it deserves at least one honest answer from both sides.
